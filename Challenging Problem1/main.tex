\large\textbf{Question\,:}\\
Let X be a random variable such that $E(X)=E(X^2)=1$. Then $E(X^{100})=?$\\
\begin{multicols}{2}
    \begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*.]
        \item 0 \\
        \item 1
        \item $2^{100}$ \\
        \item $2^{100}+1$
    \end{enumerate}
\end{multicols}
\textbf{Solution: }\\
Given $E(X)=E(X^2)=1$, hence variance of X is
\begin{align}
    Var(X) &=E(X^2)-(E(X))^2\\
     &=1-(1)^2 \\
     &=0
\end{align}

Also,
\begin{align}
    Var(X) &= E[(X-E(X))^2]
\end{align}
$\implies  E[(X-E(X))^2] = 0$\\
$\implies  X-E(X)^2=0\;\; \forall X$\\
$\implies  X= E(X)^2 =1 \;\; \forall X$\\
$\therefore$ The random variable X is a constant and equals to 1.\\
Hence,
\begin{align}
    E(X^{100}) = 1
\end{align}
Option 2 is the correct answer.
